Understanding AQI

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a standardized measure that converts concentrations of common pollutants into a single indicator ranging from 0 (Good) to 500 (Hazardous). A higher value signifies poorer air quality and increased health risks.

Regional Focus: Sabah, Sarawak, and Terengganu

We take a closer look at Sabah, Sarawak, and Terengganu to understand their air quality patterns. While Terengganu lies on the West Coast, Sabah and Sarawak are in East Malaysia (Borneo). Despite limited data coverage, these regions provide a strong representation of Malaysia’s environmental dynamics.

Weather and Pollutants

Temperature and Humidity

Pollutant levels tend to increase with rising temperature. Conversely, lower humidity levels often correspond to reduced pollution. However, temperature appears to have a stronger effect on AQI, likely due to enhanced chemical reactions in warmer air.

Wind and Pollution

When wind speed increases, pollutant levels often rise too. This suggests that strong winds may transport pollutants from other regions (a transport effect), while calmer air allows pollutants to settle and disperse locally.

Pollutant Distribution

Pollutants are relatively evenly distributed across Malaysia’s East Coast and Borneo regions. This pattern suggests that large-scale atmospheric circulation and shared climatic influences play significant roles in spreading airborne particles and gases across the nation.

Dew Point and Pollutant Concentration

A higher dew point means more moisture in the air — which can trap pollutants or help remove them through condensation. Even small annual changes in dew point seem to correspond with noticeable shifts in pollutant levels.

Conclusion

Weather conditions significantly shape Malaysia’s air quality. High temperature amplifies chemical reactions that form pollutants, while humidity and dew point affect how long they stay suspended. Wind can both spread and dilute pollutants depending on its speed and direction. Overall, the data shows that temperature and wind speed have the most consistent impact on AQI variation across the years, underscoring the need for continuous monitoring and adaptive environmental management to maintain cleaner air.
